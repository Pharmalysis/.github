# Pharmalysis

**Pharmalysis** is an independent research collective focused on pharmaceutical and chemical analysis through data, computation, and a healthy disregard for institutional gatekeeping.

Our core idea is simple:  
there is an enormous amount of publicly available biomedical literature, clinical trial data, regulatory filings, and compound information that rarely gets analyzed outside traditional pharmaceutical companies, CROs, or academic labs.

We think that’s a waste.

So we analyze it ourselves.

---

## What We Do

Pharmalysis studies pharmaceutical compounds, clinical trials, and biomedical datasets using modern computational tools — including machine learning, statistical modeling, and automated literature analysis.

Our work typically involves:

- dissecting **clinical trial outcomes**
- reviewing **drug efficacy claims**
- exploring **pharmacokinetics (PK) and pharmacodynamics (PD) signals**
- comparing compounds across published studies
- building tooling that helps researchers navigate messy biomedical data

In other words: we read the papers, pull the datasets, run the numbers, and see what actually holds up.

Sometimes the results are interesting.  
Sometimes they're disappointing.  
Occasionally they're profitable.

---

## The Philosophy

Pharmalysis is loosely inspired by the old-school style of independent drug analysis popularized by Martin Shkreli — the idea that you don't need a billion-dollar lab to critically analyze pharmaceutical science.

You just need:

- access to the data
- statistical tools
- a willingness to read very boring PDFs
- and occasionally a financial thesis

Yes, there can be **financial angles** in pharmaceutical analysis.  
Markets move when drugs succeed, fail, or get misunderstood.

But the motivation isn't purely speculative.  
Good analysis benefits everyone:

- researchers
- clinicians
- investors
- regulators
- and anyone curious about how modern medicine actually gets evaluated.

If markets happen to reward good analysis, we consider that a feature rather than a bug.

---

## Why This Exists

Pharmaceutical research is one of the most data-rich fields on earth, yet the analysis is often locked behind:

- institutional barriers  
- expensive databases  
- regulatory complexity  
- or simply lack of computational tooling.

Pharmalysis aims to experiment with a more **open, computational-first approach** to understanding drug research.

We combine:

- open scientific literature
- regulatory datasets
- AI-assisted research workflows
- reproducible code

to build a more transparent way to analyze pharmaceutical science.

---

## What Pharmalysis Is Not

Pharmalysis is **not** a pharmaceutical company.  
We do not manufacture drugs.  
We do not run clinical trials.  
We do not provide medical advice.

We analyze information.

Think of it as **computational drug intelligence**, not drug development.

---

## Current Direction

The long-term goal of Pharmalysis is to build tooling and research workflows that make large-scale pharmaceutical analysis easier.

This includes:

- automated literature parsing
- trial outcome databases
- compound comparison tools
- AI-assisted biomedical research pipelines

In short, we want to make it easier to ask questions like:

> *"Does this drug actually work as well as the paper claims?"*

and get an answer backed by data rather than marketing.
